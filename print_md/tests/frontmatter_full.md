## The Glass Garden

Mirela pressed her palm flat against the wall and felt it breathe.

Not metaphorically — the glass *exhaled*, a slow pulse that travelled up her wrist and settled behind her sternum. She had been warned about this. She had not believed it.

"Don't touch anything," Soren had said. He was already twenty feet ahead, threading between columns of suspended water that hung in the air like frozen rain.

She touched everything.

The garden had been built by someone who did not distinguish between inside and outside, between what was meant to be seen and what was meant to be felt. Every surface was a mirror angled to catch a different hour of the day. At dusk, Mirela had been told, the whole space turned the colour of a bruise.

It was not dusk. It was mid-morning, and the light came in sharp and indiscriminate, finding her wherever she tried to hide from it.
